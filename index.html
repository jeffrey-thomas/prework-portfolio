Background

Programming as a hobby

During college I had a difficult time deciding on a major. I spent a couple of years as a Computer science major, learning the basics of programming in C++ and Java. I took enough computer science classes that I ended up with a computer science minor. Despite finding programming interesting and fun I ended up deciding to be a physics teacher.

I think most people that become teachers value and enjoy learning. I continued to learn and make use of my interest in programming while maintaining my class website and preparing hands on activities for my students. I learned HTML, CSS, and Javascript to make the website as easy to navigate as possible for the students. I also wrote some simple simulations for topics like wave superposition and the doppler effect that I posted on the website. I also programmed small computers called Arduinos to collect data from devices such as thermocouples (temperature), photogates (time), and ultrasonic sonar devices (distance).

Why Change?

Covid and remote teaching

During the pandemic the teaching profession changed drastically. While most teachers and students were back in the classroom for the 2021-2022 school year, the district I worked for decided to open an online academy and transferred me to be its science teacher. This online academy used a commercial online curriculum that was already created which meant my job was mostly grading assignments from 14 different classes. I was no longer using my creativity to design and improve lessons and hands on activities and by the middle of the school year I was completely burnt out.

When it became clear that I would not be able to transfer back to an in-person school in the same district I began to consider other options. The idea of finding a job in another district was not appealing and I realized the last two years had killed my enthusiasm for being a teacher. I have always really enjoyed programming and I had kept up with it to an extent during my years as a teacher so it seems like the perfect choice for a new career.

Why Coding Temple?

Incomplete skills

While looking into programming jobs that I might be qualified for I noticed a few gaps in the skillset that I had developed when just following my own interests. While I am confident in my HTML, CSS, and javascript abilities I have little or no experience with python, databases, and server side technologies. I also realized I could use help knowing how to demonstrate the skills that I already have in a way that employers are looking for.
